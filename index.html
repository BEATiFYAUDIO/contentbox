North Star

I hit play → it plays → optional unlock. The experience is modern, but the content remains verifiable and governed by policy.

  • Local-first: your library and source content live with you.
  • P2P-ready: resolve creators on LAN first; remote options later.
  • Verifiable delivery: hash-addressed manifests enable integrity checks and caching.
  • Splits built-in: invite stakeholders, lock agreements, then publish to storefront.

Why ContentBox

Creators shouldn’t have to trade ownership, provenance, or payout routing to get instant playback and simple sharing. ContentBox is designed for creators who want control, and robust enough for teams that need a path without forcing a full platform migration.

Control the origin

Content is packaged locally into a manifest with hashes so delivery can be verified, cached, and trusted without central custody.

Links that respect rights

Links feel effortless, but access is gated by entitlement. The default message is not “free content”. It’s “verified content with optional unlock”.

Splits and royalties, native

Invite stakeholders, lock a split version, and keep royalties visible as Owned vs Invited participation. This is not a bolt-on spreadsheet.

How it works

The user story stays simple. Under the hood, ContentBox is built for verifiable delivery and frictionless playback so “link → plays” works the way people expect — without losing control of the origin.

Publish

Local content becomes a hash-addressed manifest. Policy and pricing attach to the manifest, and integrity checks support caching and verifiable delivery.

Resolve

Buyers resolve the creator LAN-first when possible. Remote resolution can be configured later. Cached host and manual entry serve as fallbacks.

Play

Playback streams with HTTP Range for instant start and seeking. Previews are optional. Entitlement tokens gate access for paid content.

Settle

Splits route value to the right people. Payments are sats/zaps oriented. During early P2P and playback hardening, a payment stub can keep the user journey intact while settlement plumbing matures.

Core flows

ContentBox is designed to make the “rights reality” obvious. Owners see what they own. Invitees see what they were granted. Store exposure follows locked agreements.

Library → choose what to play

The library stays fast and local. Networking should enhance it, not replace it. Your content remains available even when you’re offline.

Publish → verifiable manifest

Publishing produces a manifest with hashes so files can be verified at rest and in transit. This unlocks durable caching and trust.

Link → plays → unlock

Buyers open a link and playback starts quickly. Seeking works. If content is gated, entitlement unlocks access without making the creator surrender control to a platform.

Splits → invite → lock

Stakeholders are invited and approvals are collected before a split version is locked. This reduces disputes and keeps expectations aligned.

Royalties → owned vs invited

Participation is visible and auditable. Settlement follows the locked agreement, and later can be automated through sats/zaps.

Payments → sats/zaps

Payments are built for sats and zaps. Early on, the interface can be present with a stub so the product story stays consistent while infrastructure hardens.

What ships first

The sequencing stays aligned with the North Star. Playback and link-based consumption land first. A public marketplace and deeper traversal come after persistence is proven.

LAN-first playback MVP

Link opens, seller resolves, playback starts fast, seeking works. This is the foundation before adding complex networking.

Installer and onboarding

A frictionless install for Windows and Linux so “two nodes to network” becomes real in minutes.

Payments plug-in

Add sats and zaps once the flow is durable. Keep the user story consistent from the start.
